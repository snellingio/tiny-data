# aeriality

aerial aer·i·al | ˈerēəl | adjective [attributive] existing, happening, or operating in the air: an aerial battle | an intrepid aerial adventurer. • coming or carried out from the air, especially using aircraft: aerial bombardment of civilian targets | aerial photography. • (of a part of a plant) growing above ground: knobby sections of aerial roots. • (of a bird) spending much of its time in flight: the more aerial and terrestrial birds are less dependent on a strictly aquatic habitat. • of or in the atmosphere; atmospheric. noun 1 British English another term for antenna (sense 2 of the noun): jiggle the aerial on the radio. 2 (aerials) a type of maneuver in gymnastics, skiing, or surfing involving freestyle jumps or somersaults. DERIVATIVES aeriality noun aerially | ˈerēəlē | adverb ORIGIN mid 16th century: via Latin aerius from Greek aerios (from aēr ‘air’) + -al.

# aerie

aerie aer·ie | ˈerē, ˈirē | (also eyrie) noun a large nest of a bird of prey, especially an eagle, typically built high in a tree or on a cliff. ORIGIN early 16th century: from medieval Latin aeria, aerea, eyria, apparently from Anglo-Norman French eire and Old French aire, of uncertain origin; perhaps from Latin ager field, country (see acre), or perhaps from Latin area ‘level piece of ground’ (see area).

# aero

aero aer·o | ˈerō | adjective informal 1 short for aeronautical, (see aeronautics): an aero club | an aero engine. 2 short for aerodynamic: the cars have a lower, more aero front end.

# aerobatic

aerobatic aer·o·bat·ic | ˌerōˈbadik | adjective performing or involving loops, rolls, and other feats of spectacular flying in aircraft: they are one of the world's premier aerobatic display teams | aerobatic maneuvers. ORIGIN First World War: from aero- + a shortened form of acrobatic.

# aerobatics

aerobatics aer·o·bat·ics | ˌerōˈbadiks | plural noun [usually treated as singular] loops, rolls, and other feats of spectacular flying performed in one or more aircraft to entertain an audience on the ground: he still enjoys flying and doing aerobatics | [as modifier] : hundreds of people gathered to witness a spectacular aerobatics show staged by dozens of helicopters and fighter jets. ORIGIN First World War: from aero- + a shortened form of acrobatics.

# aerobe

aerobe aer·obe | ˈeˌrōb | noun a microorganism which grows in the presence of air or requires oxygen for growth. ORIGIN late 19th century: coined in French from Greek aēr + bios ‘life’.

# aerobic

DERIVATIVES aerobically | əˈrōbəklē, eˈrōbəklē | adverb ORIGIN late 19th century: from aero- + Greek bios ‘life’ + -ic.
