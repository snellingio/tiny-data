# adrenal

adrenal ad·re·nal | əˈdrēn(ə)l | adjective relating to or denoting a pair of ductless glands situated above the kidneys. Each consists of a core region (adrenal medulla) secreting epinephrine and norepinephrine, and an outer region (adrenal cortex) secreting corticosteroids: adrenal function was normal in this group of patients. (usually adrenals) noun an adrenal gland. ORIGIN late 19th century: from ad- + renal.

# Adrenalin

adrenaline a·dren·al·ine | əˈdren(ə)lən | (also adrenalin) noun a hormone secreted by the adrenal glands, especially in conditions of stress, increasing rates of blood circulation, breathing, and carbohydrate metabolism and preparing muscles for exertion. Also called epinephrine: performing live really gets your adrenaline going. • (Adrenalin) trademark the hormone epinephrine extracted from animals or prepared synthetically for medicinal purposes. ORIGIN early 20th century: from adrenal + -ine4.
