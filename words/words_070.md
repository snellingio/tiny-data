# adjacent

adjacent ad·ja·cent | əˈjās(ə)nt | adjective 1 next to or adjoining something else: adjacent rooms | the area adjacent to the fire station. 2 Geometry (of angles) having a common vertex and a common side. 3 [in combination] next to or very near a specified location: a Hollywood-adjacent town | Chicago's lakefront-adjacent public spaces. • characterized by some aspects of, or similar to, a specified concept: this film may not technically qualify as horror, but it's certainly horror-adjacent | bluegrass and bluegrass-adjacent bands and artists. # adjective

adjective ad·jec·tive | ˈajəktiv | noun Grammar a word or phrase naming an attribute, added to or grammatically related to a noun to modify or describe it. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Old French adjectif, -ive, from Latin adject- ‘added’, from the verb adicere, from ad- ‘towards’ + jacere ‘throw’. The term was originally used in the phrase noun adjective, translating Latin nomen adjectivum, a translation of Greek onoma epitheton ‘attributive name’.
