# Alabaman

# alabaster

alabaster al·a·bas·ter | ˈaləˌbastər | noun a fine-grained, translucent form of gypsum, typically white, often carved into ornaments. adjective 1 made of alabaster: the gilded alabaster tomb of Sir Anthony Browne. 2 literary like alabaster in whiteness and smoothness: her alabaster cheeks flushed with warmth. ORIGIN late Middle English: via Old French from Latin alabaster, alabastrum, from Greek alabastos, alabastros.

# alacritous

# alacrity

alacrity a·lac·ri·ty | əˈlakrədē | noun brisk and cheerful readiness: she accepted the invitation with alacrity. ORIGIN late Middle English: from Latin alacritas, from alacer ‘brisk’.
