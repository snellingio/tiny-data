# aboriginal

aboriginal ab·o·rig·i·nal | ˌabəˈrijənl | adjective 1 (Aboriginal) relating to the Indigenous peoples of Australia or their languages: she's been working with Aboriginal people for the past 40 years | Aboriginal communities | Aboriginal land rights. 2 inhabiting or existing in a land from the earliest times or from before the arrival of colonists; indigenous: the aboriginal populations in Southeast Asia. noun 1 (Aboriginal) a person belonging to one of the Indigenous peoples of Australia. 2 an aboriginal inhabitant of a place. USAGE With reference to the Indigenous peoples of Australia, formulations using the adjective Aboriginal, such as Aboriginal person or Aboriginal Australian, are now preferred to Aboriginal as a noun or Aborigine, which are avoided ORIGIN mid 17th century: from Latin aborigines ‘original inhabitants’ (see aborigine) + -al.

# aboriginality

Aboriginality Ab·o·rig·i·nal·i·ty | ˌabəˌrijəˈnalədē | noun the distinctive culture of aboriginal peoples, especially those in Australia: their music reflects their Aboriginality.

# aborigine

aborigine ab·o·rig·i·ne | ˌabəˈrijənē | noun a person, animal, or plant that has been in a country or region from earliest times. • (Aborigine) an Indigenous inhabitant of Australia; an Aboriginal person. USAGE With reference to the Indigenous peoples of Australia, the term Aborigine is now generally avoided in favor of formulations using the adjective Aboriginal, such as Aboriginal person or Aboriginal Australian ORIGIN early 16th century: originally as plural aborigines ‘original inhabitants’ (especially referring to those of Italy), from the Latin phrase ab origine ‘from the beginning’.

# abort

abort a·bort | əˈbôrt | verb [with object] 1 carry out or undergo the abortion of (a fetus): the decision to abort the fetus | [no object] : many doctors assume that women who undergo testing would choose to abort. • [no object] (of a pregnant woman or female animal) have a miscarriage: an organism that infects sheep and can cause pregnant ewes to abort. • [no object] Biology (of an embryonic organ or organism) remain undeveloped; fail to mature. 2 bring to a premature end because of a problem or fault: the pilot aborted his landing. noun mainly technical an act of aborting a flight, space mission, or other enterprise: there was an abort because of bad weather. ORIGIN mid 16th century: from Latin abort- ‘miscarried’, from aboriri, from ab- ‘away, from’ + oriri ‘be born’.

# abortifacient

abortifacient a·bor·ti·fa·cient | əˌbôrdəˈfāSHənt | Medicine adjective (chiefly of a drug) causing the premature termination of pregnancy. noun a drug that causes the premature termination of pregnancy.

# abortion

abortion a·bor·tion | əˈbôrSHən | noun 1 the deliberate termination of a human pregnancy, most often performed during the first 28 weeks of pregnancy: concerns such as abortion and euthanasia | [count noun] : illegal abortions. • Medicine the expulsion of a fetus from the uterus by natural causes before it is able to survive independently. • Biology the arrest of the development of an organ, typically a seed or fruit. 2 an object or undertaking regarded by the speaker as unpleasant or badly made or carried out. ORIGIN mid 16th century: from Latin abortio(n-), from aboriri ‘miscarry’ (see abort).

# abortionist

abortionist a·bor·tion·ist | əˈbôrSHənəst | noun derogatory 1 a person who carries out abortions (typically used to convey disapproval of abortion). 2 a person who supports the legal right of women to choose whether or not to have an abortion.

# abortive

abortive a·bor·tive | əˈbôrdiv | adjective 1 failing to produce the intended result: rebel officers led the abortive coup | the naval campaign proved abortive | an abortive attempt to secure funding. 2 Medicine (of a virus infection) failing to produce symptoms. • dated (of an organ or organism) rudimentary; arrested in development: abortive medusae. 3 rare causing or resulting in the premature termination of pregnancy: abortive techniques. DERIVATIVES abortively | əˈbôrdivlē | adverb ORIGIN Middle English (as a noun denoting a stillborn child or animal): via Old French from Latin abortivus, from aboriri ‘miscarry’ (see abort).
