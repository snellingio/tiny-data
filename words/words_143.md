# Algonkian

Algonquian Al·gon·qui·an | alˈɡäNGk(w)ēən | (Algonkian | alˈɡäNGkēən |) adjective 1 denoting or relating to a large family of Indigenous languages formerly spoken across a vast area in North America, from the Atlantic seaboard to the Great Lakes and the Great Plains, and including Ojibwe, Cree, Blackfoot, Cheyenne, and Delaware. 2 denoting or relating to the Algonquin people. noun 1 the Algonquian family of languages. 2 a speaker of any of the Algonquian languages. Algonquian is one of the largest groups of Indigenous languages in North America, including Abnaki, Mohegan, Pequot, Ojibwa, Cree, Blackfoot, Cheyenne, Fox, Menomini, and Delaware. Although the Algonquian languages are today spoken from the east coast of North America to the Rockies, the speakers are few and several of the languages are endangered. Many English words have been adopted from these languages, e.g., moccasin, moose, and toboggan ORIGIN early 19th century: from Algonquin + -ian.

# Algonquin

Algonquin Al·gon·quin | alˈɡäNGk(w)ən | (Algonkin) noun 1 a member of an Indigenous people living mainly in Canada along the Ottawa River and its tributaries and westward to the north of Lake Superior. 2 the dialect of Ojibwe spoken by the Algonquin. adjective relating to the Algonquin or their language. USAGE The terms Algonquin and Algonquian do not mean the same thing. Algonquian refers to a large family of languages, of which Algonquin is a specific member. Algonquin is also the term used for the people speaking the Algonquin language ORIGIN French, contraction of obsolete Algoumequin, from a Mi'kmaq word meaning ‘at the place of spearing fish and eels’.

# algorithm

algorithm al·go·rithm | ˈalɡəˌriT͟Həm | noun a process or set of rules to be followed in calculations or other problem-solving operations, especially by a computer: a basic algorithm for division. • (the algorithm) a data-tracking system in which an individual's internet search history and browsing habits are used to present them with similar or related material on social media or other platforms: the algorithm showed her a video by a popular fitness influencer, with a similar body shape to hers. ORIGIN late 17th century (denoting the Arabic or decimal notation of numbers): variant (influenced by Greek arithmos ‘number’) of Middle English algorism, via Old French from medieval Latin algorismus. The Arabic source, al-Ḵwārizmī ‘the man of Ḵwārizm’ (now Khiva), was a name given to the 9th-century mathematician Abū Ja‘far Muhammad ibn Mūsa, author of widely translated works on algebra and arithmetic.
