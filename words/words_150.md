# allantoic

allantois al·lan·to·is | əˈlantəwəs | noun (plural allantoides | əˈlantəwədēz, ˈalənˈtoidēz |) the fetal membrane lying below the chorion in many vertebrates, formed as an outgrowth of the embryo's gut. In birds and reptiles it grows to surround the embryo; in eutherian mammals it forms part of the placenta. DERIVATIVES allantoic | ˈˌalənˈˌtōik, alənˈtəo͝oik | adjective allantoid | əˈlantoid, əˈlantoid | adjective ORIGIN mid 17th century: modern Latin, based on Greek allantoeidēs ‘sausage-shaped’.

# allantoin

allantoin al·lan·to·in | əˈlantōən | noun Biochemistry a crystalline compound formed in the nitrogen metabolism of many mammals (excluding primates). A cyclic compound related to hydantoin; chemical formula: C4H6N4O3 ORIGIN mid 19th century: from allantois (because it was discovered in the allantoic fluid of cows) + -in1.

# allege

allege al·lege | əˈlej | verb [reporting verb] claim or assert that someone has done something illegal or wrong, typically without proof that this is the case: [with clause] : he alleged that he had been assaulted | he is alleged to have assaulted five men | [with object and infinitive] : the offenses are alleged to have been committed outside the woman's home. • (usually be alleged) suppose or affirm to be the case: the first artifact ever alleged to be from Earhart's aircraft.
