# acriflavine

acriflavine ac·ri·fla·vine | ˌakrəˈflāvēn | noun a bright orange-red dye derived from acridine, used as an antiseptic. ORIGIN early 20th century: formed irregularly from acridine + Latin flavus ‘yellow’ + -ine4.

# acrimony

acrimony ac·ri·mo·ny | ˈakrəˌmōnē | noun bitterness or ill feeling: a quagmire of lawsuits, acrimony, and finger-pointing. ORIGIN mid 16th century (in the sense ‘bitter taste or smell’): from French acrimonie or Latin acrimonia, from acer, acri- ‘pungent, acrid’.

# acrobat

acrobat ac·ro·bat | ˈakrəˌbat | noun an entertainer who performs spectacular gymnastic feats: there were puppeteers, circus acrobats, and exotic animals on hand to provide entertainment | spectators can expect acts including world-class acrobats, aerialists, and jugglers. # acrocyanosis

acrocyanosis ac·ro·cy·a·no·sis | ˌakrōˌsīəˈnōsəs | noun Medicine bluish or purple coloring of the hands and feet caused by slow circulation. ORIGIN late 19th century: from Greek akron ‘tip’ + cyanosis.

# acromegalic

acromegaly ac·ro·meg·a·ly | ˌakrōˈmeɡəlē | noun Medicine a condition characterized by overgrowth of bone and soft tissues, most noticeably in the hands, feet, and face, caused by excessive secretion of growth hormone (most commonly by a pituitary tumor). DERIVATIVES acromegalic | ˈˌakrōməˈˌɡalik, -miˈɡalik | adjective ORIGIN late 19th century: coined in French from Greek akron ‘tip, extremity’ + megas, megal- ‘great’.
