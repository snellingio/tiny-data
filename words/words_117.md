# agonizingly

agonizingly ag·o·niz·ing·ly | ˈaɡəˌnīziNGlē | (British English also agonisingly) adverb [often as submodifier] so as to cause great physical or mental pain: progress proved agonizingly slow | the result is agonizingly bad | his muscles protested agonizingly as he pushed himself out of the chair.

# agony

agony ag·o·ny | ˈaɡənē | noun (plural agonies) extreme physical or mental suffering: he crashed to the ground in agony. • the final stages of a difficult or painful death: his last agony | the death agony. ORIGIN late Middle English (originally denoting mental anguish alone): via Old French and late Latin from Greek agōnia, from agōn ‘contest’. The sense of physical suffering dates from the early 17th century.

# agora

agora 1 ag·o·ra | ˈaɡərə | noun (plural agorae) (plural or agoras) (in ancient Greece) a public open space used for assemblies and markets. ORIGIN from Greek.

# agoraphobia

agoraphobia ag·o·ra·pho·bi·a | ˌaɡ(ə)rəˈfōbēə | noun extreme or irrational fear of entering open or crowded places, of leaving one's own home, or of being in places from which escape is difficult: he suffers from crippling agoraphobia, which prevents him from going outside. ORIGIN late 19th century: from Greek agora ‘place of assembly, marketplace’ + -phobia.

# agouti

agouti a·gou·ti | əˈɡo͞odē | noun (plural agouti) (plural or agoutis | əˈɡo͞odēz |) a large long-legged burrowing rodent related to the guinea pig, native to Central and South America. Genera Cuniculus and Dasyprocta, family Dasyproctidae: several species • fur in which each hair has alternate dark and light bands, producing a grizzled appearance. • a rodent, especially a mouse, having agouti fur. ORIGIN mid 16th century: via French or from Spanish aguti, from Tupi akutí.

# Agra

Agra A·gra | ˈäɡrə | a city on the Jumna River in Uttar Pradesh state, northern India; population 1,638,200 (est. 2009). The capital of the Mogul empire 1566–1658, it is the site of the Taj Mahal.

# agrammatism

agrammatism | əˈɡraməˌtizəm | noun Medicine a tendency to form sentences without the correct inflectional structure as a result of brain damage, as in Broca's aphasia.

# agranulocytosis

agranulocytosis a·gran·u·lo·cy·to·sis | əˌɡranyələˌsīˈtōsəs, āˌɡranyələˌsīˈtōsəs | noun Medicine a deficiency of granulocytes in the blood, causing increased vulnerability to infection. ORIGIN early 20th century: from a-1 + granulocyte + -osis.

# agraphia

agraphia | əˈɡrafēə | noun Medicine inability to write letters, symbols, words, or sentences, resulting from damage to various parts of the brain. ORIGIN mid 19th century: from a-1 ‘without’ + Greek -graphia ‘writing’.

# agrarian

agrarian a·grar·i·an | əˈɡrerēən | adjective relating to cultivated land or the cultivation of land: Brazil is rapidly diversifying its agrarian economy. • relating to landed property: the agrarian reforms. noun a person who advocates a redistribution of landed property, especially as part of a social movement. DERIVATIVES agrarianism | əˈɡrerēəˌnizəm | noun ORIGIN early 17th century (originally denoting a Roman law for the division of conquered lands): from Latin agrarius, from ager, agr- ‘field’.
