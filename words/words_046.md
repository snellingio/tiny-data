# Achinese

Achinese | ˌäCHəˈnēz, ˌaCHəˈnēz | noun (plural Achinese) 1 a member of a people living in northern Sumatra. 2 the Indonesian language of the Achinese. adjective relating to the Achinese or their language. ORIGIN from Acheh, Atjeh, a territory in northern Sumatra, + -n- + -ese.

# achlorhydria

achlorhydria a·chlor·hy·dri·a | ˌāklôrˈhīdrēə | noun Medicine absence of hydrochloric acid in the gastric secretions. ORIGIN late 19th century: from a-1 ‘without’ + chlor- + hydro- + -ia1.

# achondrite

achondrite | ˌāˈkänˌdrīt | noun a stony meteorite containing no small mineral granules (chondrules). DERIVATIVES achondritic adjective ORIGIN early 20th century: from a-1 ‘without’ + chondrite.

# achondroplasia

achondroplasia a·chon·dro·pla·sia | ˌāˌkändrəˈplāZH(ē)ə | noun a hereditary condition in which the growth of long bones is restricted by ossification of cartilage, resulting in very short limbs and sometimes a face that is small in relation to the skull. ORIGIN late 19th century: from a-1 ‘without’ + Greek khondros ‘cartilage’ + plasis ‘molding’ + -ia1.

# achondroplastic

achondroplastic a·chon·dro·plas·tic | ˌāˌkändrəˈplastik | (also achondroplasic) adjective relating to or affected by the hereditary condition achondroplasia: achondroplastic dwarfism.

# achromatic

achromatic ach·ro·mat·ic | ˌakrəˈmadik | adjective 1 relating to, employing, or denoting lenses that transmit light without separating it into constituent colors: achromatic microscopes | a lens of achromatic construction. 2 literary without color: achromatic gloom. DERIVATIVES achromatically | ˌakrəˈmadək(ə)lē | adverb achromaticity noun achromatism noun ORIGIN mid 18th century: from Greek akhrōmatos ‘colourless’ (from a- ‘without’ + khrōma, khrōmat- ‘color’) + -ic.
