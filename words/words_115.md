# Agnatha

Agnatha Ag·na·tha | ˈaɡnəTHə | plural noun Zoology a group of primitive jawless vertebrates which includes the lampreys, hagfishes, and many fossil fishlike forms. Compare with cyclostome Superclass Agnatha: the living forms are in the classes Myxini (hagfishes) and Cephalaspidomorphi (lampreys) DERIVATIVES agnathan noun, adjective ORIGIN from modern Latin Agnatha, from a-1 ‘without’ + Greek gnathos ‘jaw’.

# Agnes

Agnes, St. 1 Ag·nes, St. | ˌsānt ˈaɡnəs | (died c.304), Roman martyr. The patron saint of virgins, her emblem is a lamb (Latin agnus). Feast day, January 21.

# agnize

agnize | aɡˈnīz | (British English also agnise) verb [with object] archaic recognize the existence of. ORIGIN mid 16th century: from Latin agnoscere ‘acknowledge, recognize’, on the pattern of cognize, recognize.

# agnomen

agnomen ag·no·men | aɡˈnōmən | noun a fourth name occasionally given as an honor to an ancient Roman citizen. Compare with nomen,, cognomen,, praenomen: he earned the agnomen “Coriolanus” by conspicuous bravery in battle. ORIGIN mid 17th century: Latin, from ad ‘to’ + gnomen, nomen ‘name’.

# agnosia

agnosia ag·no·sia | aɡˈnōZHə | noun Medicine inability to interpret sensations and hence to recognize things, typically as a result of brain damage: visual agnosia. ORIGIN late 19th century: coined in German from Greek agnōsia ‘ignorance’.
