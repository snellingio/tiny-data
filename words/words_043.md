# acetate

acetate ac·e·tate | ˈasəˌtāt | noun 1 Chemistry a salt or ester of acetic acid, containing the anion CH3COO− or the group —OCCH3. 2 cellulose acetate, especially as used to make textile fibers or plastic: [as modifier] : acetate silk. • a transparency made of cellulose acetate film. • a recording disk coated with cellulose acetate. ORIGIN late 18th century: from acetic acid + -ate1.

# acetic

acetic a·ce·tic | əˈsēdik | adjective of or like vinegar or acetic acid. ORIGIN late 18th century: from French acétique, from Latin acetum ‘vinegar’.

# Acetobacter

acetobacter a·ce·to·bac·ter | əˈsēdəˌbaktər, ˈasədəˌbaktər | noun bacteria that oxidize organic compounds to acetic acid, as in vinegar formation. Genus Acetobacter; Gram-negative oval or rod-shaped bacteria ORIGIN modern Latin (genus name), from aceto- + bacterium.

# acetone

acetone ac·e·tone | ˈasəˌtōn | noun Chemistry a colorless volatile liquid ketone made by oxidizing isopropanol, used as an organic solvent and synthetic reagent. Chemical formula: CH3COCH3 ORIGIN mid 19th century: from acetic acid + -one.

# acetous

acetous ac·e·tous | əˈsēdəs, ˈasədəs | adjective producing or resembling vinegar: acetous fermentation. ORIGIN late Middle English (rare before the late 18th century): from late Latin acetosus ‘sour’, from Latin acetum ‘vinegar’.
