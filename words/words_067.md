# Adelaide

Adelaide Ad·e·laide | ˈadlˌād | a city in southern Australia, the capital and chief port of the state of South Australia; population 1,172,105 (2008).

# adenine

adenine ad·e·nine | ˈadnˌēn | noun Biochemistry a compound which is one of the four constituent bases of nucleic acids. A purine derivative, it is paired with thymine in double-stranded DNA. Alternative name: 6-aminopurine; chemical formula: C5H5N5 ORIGIN late 19th century: coined in German from Greek adēn ‘gland’ + -ine4.

# adenoid

adenoids ad·e·noids | ˈadnˌoidz | plural noun a mass of enlarged lymphatic tissue between the back of the nose and the throat, often hindering speaking and breathing in young children. DERIVATIVES adenoidal | ˌadnˈoid(ə)l | adjective ORIGIN late 19th century: adenoid from Greek adēn ‘gland’ + -oid.
