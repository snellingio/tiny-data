# adyton

adytum ad·y·tum | ˈadədəm | (also adyton) noun (plural adyta | ˈadədə, ˈadətə |) the innermost sanctuary of an ancient Greek temple. ORIGIN Latin, from Greek aduton, neuter singular of adutos ‘impenetrable’, from a- ‘not’ + duein ‘enter’.

# Aegean

Aegean Ae·ge·an | əˈjēən, āˈjēən | adjective relating to the region comprising the Aegean Sea and its coasts and islands. (the Aegean) noun the Aegean Sea or its region. ORIGIN early 16th century (in Scots): via Latin from Greek Aigaios, from the name of Aegeus, who is said in some stories to have jumped into the sea and drowned in the mistaken belief that his son Theseus had died fighting the Minotaur: see + -ean.

# aegis

ORIGIN late Middle English (in aegis (sense 2 of the noun)): via Latin from Greek aigis ‘shield of Zeus’.
