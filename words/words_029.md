# acari

Acari Ac·a·ri | ˈakəˌrī, ˈakəˌrē | (Acarina | ˌakəˈrīnə, ˌakəˈrēnə |) plural noun Zoology a large order (or subclass) of small arachnids which comprises the mites and ticks. They are distinguished by an apparent lack of body divisions. DERIVATIVES acarid | ˈakərəd | noun, adjective ORIGIN modern Latin (plural), from acarus, from Greek akari ‘mite’.

# acaricide

acaricide a·car·i·cide | əˈkerəˌsīd | noun a substance poisonous to mites or ticks. ORIGIN mid 19th century: from modern Latin acarus ‘mite, tick’ + -cide.
