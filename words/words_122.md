# aigrette

aigrette ai·grette | āˈɡret | noun a headdress consisting of a white egret's feather or other decoration such as a spray of gems. ORIGIN mid 17th century: French, ‘egret’.

# aiguille

aiguille ai·guille | āˈɡwēl | noun a sharp pinnacle of rock in a mountain range. ORIGIN late 17th century (denoting an obelisk): from French, literally ‘needle’, from late Latin acicula, acucula ‘little needle’, diminutive of Latin acus.

# aiguillette

aiguillette ai·guil·lette | ˌāɡwəˈlet | noun an ornament on some military and naval uniforms, consisting of braided loops hanging from the shoulder and on dress uniforms ending in points. ORIGIN late 18th century: from French, literally ‘small needle’, diminutive of aiguille. The French word was borrowed earlier into Middle English as aglet, a tag on the end of a lace.

# ail

ail | āl | verb [with object] archaic trouble or afflict (someone) in mind or body: exercise is good for whatever ails you. ORIGIN Old English eglian, eglan, from egle ‘troublesome’, of Germanic origin; related to Gothic agls ‘disgraceful’.

# Ailanthus

ailanthus ai·lan·thus | āˈlanTHəs | noun a tall large-leaved deciduous tree that is widely grown as an ornamental or shade tree. Native to Asia and Australasia, it has been naturalized in North America and central and southern Europe. Genus Ailanthus, family Simaroubaceae: several species, in particular the tree of heaven ORIGIN modern Latin, from French ailante, from Ambonese ailanto, literally ‘tree of heaven’ (the ending being influenced by names ending with -anthus, from Greek anthos ‘flower’).

# aileron

aileron ai·ler·on | ˈāləˌrän | noun a hinged surface in the trailing edge of an airplane wing, used to control lateral balance. ORIGIN early 20th century: from French, literally ‘small wing’, diminutive of aile, from Latin ala ‘wing’.
