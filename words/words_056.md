# acrylic

acrylic a·cryl·ic | əˈkrilik | adjective (of synthetic resins and textile fibers) made from polymers of acrylic acid or acrylates: a red acrylic sweater. • relating to or denoting paints based on acrylic resin as a medium: acrylic colors | an acrylic painting. noun 1 an acrylic textile fiber: a sweater in four-ply acrylic. 2 (often acrylics) an acrylic paint: washes of white acrylic. ORIGIN mid 19th century: from the liquid aldehyde acrolein (from Latin acer, acri- ‘pungent’ + ol(eum) ‘oil’ + -in1)+ -yl + -ic.
