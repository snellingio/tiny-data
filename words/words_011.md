# ablution

ablution ab·lu·tion | əˈblo͞oSHən | (usually ablutions) noun formal, or humorous the act of washing oneself (often used for humorously formal effect): the women performed their ablutions. • a ceremonial act of washing parts of the body or sacred containers. DERIVATIVES ablutionary | əˈblo͞oSHəˌnerē | adjective ORIGIN late Middle English: from Latin ablutio(n-), from abluere, from ab- ‘away’ + luere ‘wash’. The original use was as a term in chemistry and alchemy meaning ‘purification by using liquids’, hence ‘purification of the body by washing’ (mid 16th century).

# Abnaki

Abenaki Ab·e·na·ki | ˌabəˈnakē, ˌäbəˈnäkē, ˌabəˈnäkē | (Abnaki | ˌabˈnakē, ˌäbˈnäkē, ˌabˈnäkē |) noun (plural Abenaki or plural Abenakis) 1 a member of an Indigenous people of North America living mainly in Quebec and Maine, divided into the Eastern Abenaki and Western Abenaki. 2 either or both of the Algonquian languages of the Eastern Abenaki and Western Abenaki. adjective relating to the Abenaki or their language. ORIGIN from French Abénaqui, from Montagnais ouabanăkionek ‘people of the eastern land’.
