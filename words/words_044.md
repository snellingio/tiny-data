# acetylcholine

acetylcholine a·ce·tyl·cho·line | ˌasədlˈkōˌlīn | noun Biochemistry a compound which occurs throughout the nervous system, in which it functions as a neurotransmitter.

# acetylene

acetylene a·cet·y·lene | əˈsedələn, əˈsedəˌlēn | noun Chemistry a colorless pungent-smelling hydrocarbon gas, which burns with a bright flame, used in welding and formerly in lighting. Alternative name: ethyne; chemical formula: C2H2 ORIGIN mid 19th century: from acetic acid + -yl + -ene.

# acetylide

acetylide a·cet·y·lide | əˈsedlˌīd | noun Chemistry a compound formed from acetylene and a metal, containing the anion (C≡C)2− or HC≡C−. Acetylides are typically unstable or explosive.

# ach

ach | äk, äKH | exclamation mainly Scottish English used to express a range of emotions including surprise, pleasure, sympathy, and regret: ach well, win some lose some. ORIGIN late 15th century: from Scottish Gaelic, Dutch, and German; ultimately imitative. Compare with ah.

# Achaean

Achaean A·chae·an | əˈkēən | adjective relating to Achaea in ancient Greece. • literary (especially in Homeric contexts) Greek. noun an inhabitant of Achaea. • literary (especially in Homeric contexts) a Greek. The Achaeans were among the earliest Greek-speaking inhabitants of Greece, being established there well before the 12th century BC. Some scholars identify them with the Mycenaeans of the 14th–13th centuries BC. The Greek protagonists in the Trojan War are regularly called Achaeans in the Iliad, though this may have referred only to the leaders

# Achaemenian

# achalasia

achalasia ach·a·la·sia | ˌākəˈlāZH(ē)ə | noun Medicine a condition in which the muscles of the lower part of the esophagus fail to relax, preventing food from passing into the stomach. ORIGIN early 20th century: from a-1 ‘without, not’ + Greek khalasis ‘loosening’ (from khalan ‘relax’) + -ia1.

# achar

achar | əˈCHär | (also achaar) noun (in South Asian cooking) a type of relish in which the food is preserved in spiced oil: mango achar. ORIGIN from Hindi acār, from Persian.

# Achates

Achates A·cha·tes | əˈkādēz | Greek mythology Roman mythology a companion of Aeneas. His loyalty to his friend was so exemplary as to become proverbial, hence the term fidus Achates (‘faithful Achates’).
