# adhibit

adhibit ad·hib·it | adˈhibət | verb (adhibits) (, adhibiting | adˈhibədiNG |) (, adhibited | adˈhibədəd |) [with object] formal apply or affix (something) to something else: signed by a partner who would either adhibit the firm's signature or his own. DERIVATIVES adhibition | ˌad(h)əˈbiSHən, adhiˈbiSH(ə)n | noun ORIGIN early 16th century (in the sense ‘take in, include’): from Latin adhibit- ‘brought in’, from the verb adhibere, from ad- ‘to’ + habere ‘hold, have’.

# adiabatic

DERIVATIVES adiabatically | ˈˌāˌdīəˈbadək(ə)lē, ˈˌadiəˈbadək(ə)lē | adverb ORIGIN mid 19th century: from Greek adiabatos ‘impassable’, from a- ‘not’ + dia ‘through’ + batos ‘passable’ (from bainein ‘go’), + -ic.
