# agronomic

DERIVATIVES agronomic | ˌaɡrəˈnämik | adjective agronomical | ˈˌaɡrəˈnäməkəl, aɡrəˈnɒmik(ə)l | adjective agronomically | ˈˌaɡrəˈnämək(ə)lē, aɡrəˈnɒmik(ə)lē | adverb ORIGIN late 18th century: from French agronomie, from agronome ‘agriculturist’, from Greek agros ‘field’ + -nomos ‘arranging’ (from nemoein ‘arrange’).

# agronomics

agronomics | ˌaɡrəˈnämiks | plural noun [often treated as singular] the science of soil management and crop production. ORIGIN mid 19th century: from French agronomique, from agronome ‘agriculturist’ (see agronomy) + -ics.

# agrostology

agrostology ag·ros·tol·o·gy | ˌaɡrəˈstäləjē | noun the branch of botany concerned with grasses. ORIGIN mid 19th century: from Greek agrōstis (denoting a kind of grass) + -logy.

# aground

aground a·ground | əˈɡround | adjective [predicative] (of a ship) lying on or touching the ground under shallow water: a cargo ship aground in the Mediterranean. adverb (with reference to a ship) on or on to the ground under shallow water: the ships must slow to avoid running aground. ORIGIN Middle English (in the sense ‘on the ground’): from a-2 ‘on’ + ground1.
